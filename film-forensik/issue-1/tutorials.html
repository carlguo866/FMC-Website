The Beginning of My Journey
 Henry Hunt
Nov 10, 2018
When I started taking pictures, I did not really think of it as photography. I thought of it more as a way of documenting cool things that I may want to look back on in the future. As I kept taking more and more photos of the things I loved, I began to suspect I may have an interest in photography. I went through some of the drawers in my house and found an old camera that my parents used on their honeymoon. I began taking photos with it and was surprised at how well the photos turned out given I had never used a professional camera before. For 2 years I used the camera to take pictures of anything that interested me, but in the Summer of 2017, I felt like it was time for an upgrade. I purchased a Nikon D40 and set to work. The photos it produced were incredible and I truly realized how an amateur photographer could really take stunning photos with entry-level equipment. So I encourage you, whether you have been taking photos on your phone of everything or just pulling it out every now and then to take a picture of something you think is neat, to think a little more about your shots. Think about placement and angles. About lighting. Edit them a little. You will truly be surprised and impressed with yourself at how easy it is to produce great photos with a minimal amount of equipment and software.
