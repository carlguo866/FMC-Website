Second, tax haven is not a really bad thing. According to Daniel Mitchell, there is a unfortunate tendency of vote-seeking governments to behave like “stationary bandits,” seeking to extract the maximum amount of money from taxpayers. (i.e., the revenue-maximizing tax rate). Tax competition is a way of discouraging this self-destructive behavior. Politicians are less likely to over-tax and over-spend if they know that jobs and investment can migrate from high-tax nations to low-tax jurisdictions. Offshore banking is also a significant protection against tyranny and unjust autocracy. Tax havens make it harder for autocratic governments to confiscate resources from their citizens.
