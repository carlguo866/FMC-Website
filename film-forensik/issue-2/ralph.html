Ralph Breaks the Internet
 Alex Zhou
Jan 5, 2019
Director: Phil Johnston and Rich Moore
Leads: John C. Reilly, Sarah Silverman, Gal Gaddot
Length: 112 minutes
Rotten Tomato: 86%
IMDB: 7.8



In "Ralph Breaks the Internet," video-game bad guy Ralph and best friend Vanellope leave the comforts of Litwak's arcade in an attempt to save Vanellope’s game, Sugar Rush. Their quest takes them to the vast, uncharted world of the internet where they rely on the citizens of the internet--the Netizens--to help navigate their way. Lending a virtual hand are Yesss, the head algorithm and the heart and soul of the trend-making site "BuzzzTube," and Shank, a tough-as-nails driver from a gritty online auto-racing game called Slaughter Race, a place Vanellope wholeheartedly embraces--so much so that Ralph worries he may lose the only friend he's ever had.

I would say that Ralph breaks the internet is one of the best children movies Disney had made in the past five years. With the director and production team of Zootopia, it perfectly continues the already excellent-designed storyline from the first movie of the series, Wreck it Ralph.For this new chapter, all elements of old fashion video games that everyone loves from the last movie come back to the spotlight, along with many new features of more well-known matters like Ebay and Youtube. It is indeed very wise of Disney to stop focusing on the story of vintage entertainments, but moves its target towards the modern amusements from the online digital world. It makes the story both refreshing and tightly bonded to the focus of current society. Moreover, Ralph breaks the internet also has logical narratives, good quality jokes, warm themes, and brilliant depth in characters which weren’t fully discovered in the former movie. This is especially precious since multiple rather recent-made Disney films had failed to capture these necessary elements and caused great disappointments. Ralph breaks the internet absolutely reverse that. In addition, while the relationship between Ralph and Vanellope develops and changes throughout their new journey, the movie touches base on complex topics that are truly inspiring to both adults and children, like what a healthy friendship really means, how female were portrait in society, how to face one’s dream and so on. It definitely overachieved Disney children movies’ goal: being entertaining while highly educational. Therefore, Ralph breaks the internet meets most of my standards for a Disney movie. It maintains or even exceeds its predecessor, and is definitely worth people of all age to appreciate, gain joy, and have different takeaways from it.
